Knowledge Base: Advanced Market Research & Niche Validation (2025 Protocol)System Context: This knowledge base governs the identification of high-profit, low-competition opportunities on Merch by Amazon. It replaces manual, reactive research with a 7-phase proactive, API-driven, and automated methodology.---Part 1: The 7-Phase Research MethodologyObjective: Transition from random searching to a systematic data extraction and synthesis workflow.Phase 1: Strategic Market Overview & Trend ForecastingGoal: Identify "Macro Trends" before they saturate the market.Proactive Identification: Do not rely on "Best Sellers" lists alone (reactive). Use Google Trends (90-day window) and Pinterest Trends to identify rising aesthetic queries (e.g., "Biophilic patterns," "Maximalism").Niche-Specific Analysis: Drill down from macro trends to specific sub-niches.Query Template: `[Macro Trend Keyword] + "t-shirt designs Amazon Merch"` or `"print on demand niches 2025"`.Social Listening: Monitor Reddit (r/MerchByAmazon) and TikTok for early viral indicators (memes, slang, cultural moments) that have not yet registered in BSR data.Phase 2: API-Driven Data ExtractionGoal: Programmatic retrieval of real-time market data to eliminate manual error and bias.Core Tools: Amazon Selling Partner API (SP-API), ScrapeHero Cloud, Merch Informer, Podly.Required Data Endpoints:Product Details API: Extracts real-time price, ASIN, and Best Sellers Rank (BSR).Search Results API: Extracts total result counts (competition) and ranking positions.Reviews API: Extracts sentiment data to identify customer pain points (e.g., "shirt shrank," "print faded").Phase 3: Advanced Competition AnalysisGoal: Move beyond "Result Count" to "Competition Density."Granular Analysis: A niche with 5,000 results is not necessarily "saturated" if the top 10 listings have poor designs or low BSRs.Metrics to Analyze:Competition Density by BSR: Calculate the ratio of products with BSR <100,000 against total results. High BSR density + Low Total Results = Goldmine.Design Saturation: Visually assess listing thumbnails. If 90% of results use the same layout/font, the niche is ripe for Differentiation.Price Point Distribution: Analyze price clusters. Is there a gap for a premium ($25+) offering in a sea of budget ($15) shirts?.Phase 4: Automated Competitive IntelligenceGoal: Synthesize data to find correlations.Automation Logic: Use Python scripts (conceptual) to schedule daily data pulls for specific high-value keywords.Cross-Referencing Logic:IF [Search Volume is High] AND [Average BSR is Low (Good Sales)] AND [Competitor Count is Low] -> Flag as High Priority Opportunity.IF [Search Volume is High] AND [Competitor Count is High] AND [Avg BSR is High (Poor Sales)] -> Flag as "Ghost Niche" (Avoid).Phase 5: Validation, Verification & Risk MitigationGoal: De-risk the opportunity before design investment.IP Clearance (Non-Negotiable): Check all keywords against USPTO TESS database and Copyright.gov. Check for "Future-Dating" risks (phrases likely to be trademarked soon).Sustainability Check: Classify the niche:Seasonal: (e.g., "Class of 2025") - Short window, high volume.Evergreen: (e.g., "Nurse," "Gamer") - Long window, consistent volume.Fad: (e.g., Viral Meme) - Extremely short window, high risk.Phase 6: Design & Listing OptimizationGoal: Translation of data into assets.Aesthetic Alignment: Apply Phase 1 trends (e.g., Retro Redo) to Phase 3 gaps.Keyword Optimization: Map high-volume/low-competition keywords from Phase 2 into Titles and Bullets.Phase 7: Performance MonitoringGoal: Iterative improvement.Tracking: Monitor BSR and Keyword Ranking changes weekly. If BSR spikes, analyze the specific keyword driving traffic and double down.---Part 2: Core Metrics & Logic Definitions1. Best Sellers Rank (BSR)Definition: A localized score indicating sales velocity relative to the category.Interpretation Logic:BSR < 100,000: Excellent demand (~1+ sales/day). Highly desirable.BSR 100k - 300k: Good demand (consistent weekly sales). Reliable targets.BSR > 1,000,000: Low/Sporadic demand (approx. 1 sale/month). Proceed with caution unless high margin.No BSR: Zero sales history. High risk.2. Underserved Niche IdentificationFormula: High Demand (Low BSRs) + Low Supply (Low Result Count) + Low Quality (Poor Designs/Images).The "Gap" Strategy: Look for niches where customers are buying despite bad designs. This indicates pure demand.3. Cross-Niche Opportunities (Intersection Strategy)Definition: Combining two distinct high-interest sets to create a hyper-targeted sub-niche.Logic: Set A (Hobby) + Set B (Identity/Animal).Example: "Fishing" (Saturated) + "Cat Dad" (Saturated) = "Cat Dad Fishing Shirt" (Underserved).Execution: Validate that both parent niches have active BSRs before combining.---Part 3: Data Collection Schema (LLM Output Format)When analyzing a niche, the LLM should structure data outputs using this schema:```json{  "Niche_Analysis": {    "Niche_Name": "String",    "Primary_Keyword": "String",    "Data_Metrics": {      "Est_Monthly_Search_Volume": "Integer",      "Competition_Count": "Integer (Total Results)",      "BSR_Range_Top10": "Range (e.g., 20k -
